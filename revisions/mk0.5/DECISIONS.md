# mk0.5 Engineering Decisions

## Expanded base instead of wider tower

The tower body remains close to mk0.4 dimensions. Only the base footprint grows, so module trays, rails and service access stay compatible.

## Ribs instead of a thick slab

The base uses a central intake cutout and ribs around the fan and perimeter. This keeps the part printable in PETG without pretending that mass alone solves stiffness.

## Screw-based service stop

The Mini PC tray uses a slot and screw/washer stop. This avoids a PETG latch fatigue point and makes deliberate removal possible during service.

## Rear spine as structural service channel

The rear spine remains a cable channel, but mk0.5 adds wall thickness, ribs, mount tabs and a screw-mounted cover. The goal is a semi-box structure tied to the top and bottom frame.

## Sectional shear panels

The side panels remain sectional. Lower and upper sections become structural shear panels, while the middle section remains more service-oriented.

## Torsion brace kit prepared, not required

mk0.5 adds mounting points for a future diagonal brace kit but does not make it part of the baseline revision.
